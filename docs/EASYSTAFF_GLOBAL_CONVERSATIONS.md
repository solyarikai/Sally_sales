## SDR RESPONSE PATTERNS (what worked vs what didn't)

### Best SDR Responses:
1. **Qualifying question** (used in 8/28 email threads): "How many freelancers do you have and where they located?" — extracts deal size immediately
2. **One-invoice explanation**: "You get one single invoice from us, and make one single payment. Your work is done." — simplest value prop, used in Achal Gupt (led to meeting) and Deepak Baghel
3. **Hybrid EOR pivot** (Adam Naser): "Are you open to a hybrid proposal where we handle senior staff as B2B contractors (saving ~30% on EOR fees)?" — turned RFP into broader deal
4. **Language switch** (Alexey Bugrov, Jemmy Ganoza): SDR adapted to Russian/Spanish mid-conversation
5. **Time slot offers**: Always 2 specific slots ("Monday 16:00 or Tuesday 14:00 Dubai time") — reduces friction

### SDR Mistakes:
1. **Too many follow-ups without new value** (Michael Cofone): 5 follow-ups over 6 weeks, each repeating the same pitch. No new information, case studies, or content shared.
2. **No calendar link** (multiple leads): Proposing times via text instead of sending a booking link. Achal Gupt, Michael Cofone, and others could have been booked faster.
3. **Wrong name** (James Martin thread): SDR addressed James as "Lawry" — caught and apologized, lead was gracious.
4. **Missing Telegram option** (all threads): Only Email 4 mentions LinkedIn switch, but 2 leads independently asked for Telegram. Should mention TG proactively.
