ve# EasyStaff Global — City Expansion Strategy

## The Frizzon Insight (March 2026)

Frizzon Studios — Indian founders in Dubai, creative agency, 10-20 people. They booked a call because they need to pay contractors in **Lebanon, EU, and other scattered countries**. Eleonora's feedback:

> "Основатель и сотрудники индусы, сидят в дубаи, платить надо то в Ливан, то в ЕС, сталкивались со сложностями выплат через банк в разные страны"
>
> "Если брать частный кейс ОАЭ – Индия/Пакистан, то там простой банковский дешевый перевод и поэтому мы там не нужны"

**Translation of the core insight:** UAE→India/Pakistan = cheap bank transfer = EasyStaff NOT needed. The pain is **diverse, difficult corridors** where banks charge $25-50/wire + 1.5-4% FX markup + intermediary fees. For a $500 freelancer payment, that's $50-70 lost per transaction (10-14%).

This changes everything about how we prioritize cities.

---

## The Real Pain Formula

**EasyStaff wins where ALL THREE conditions are true:**

1. **Agency density is high** — enough targets to scrape (>500 agencies with 5-200 employees)
2. **Contractor geography is DIVERSE** — agency pays to 3+ countries, not just one easy corridor
3. **Local banking fails on small amounts** — wire fees eat 5-10% of a $500-1000 payment

**EasyStaff LOSES where:**
- Single-corridor payments that banks handle cheaply (UAE→India, SG→Philippines)
- Agency already uses Deel and is satisfied (rare — Deel is $49/contractor/month)
- Payments are intra-SEPA (EU→EU is free and instant)
- Agency is too large (>200 people, has in-house payroll)

## Competitor Reality Check: Deel

**Deel contractor management: $49/contractor/month** ([source](https://www.gloroots.com/blog/deel-pricing))

| Scenario | Deel cost/month | Bank cost/month | EasyStaff opportunity |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| 5 contractors | $245 | $125-350 (wires) | Medium — both are expensive |
| 15 contractors | $735 | $375-1,050 | HIGH — Deel feels expensive, banks are worse |
| 30 contractors | $1,470 | $750-2,100 | HIGHEST — any cheaper alternative wins |
| 50 contractors | $2,450 | $1,250-3,500 | Deel offers volume discounts here |

**Sweet spot: 10-30 contractors.** Deel is too expensive per head, banks are impractical, and the agency is too small for Deel to offer volume discounts. This is EasyStaff's kill zone.

**Where Deel is weakest:**
- Price-sensitive SMBs (agencies with tight margins)
- Non-English markets where Deel's sales team doesn't reach (Saudi Arabia, Qatar)
- Agencies that only need help with 3-5 "weird" corridors but Deel forces per-head pricing for ALL contractors
- Markets where Deel's brand isn't established yet

| **UK** | £15-25 | 1.5-3% | £10-20 | $50-80 | **5-8%** | UK bank fee schedules |
| **Singapore** | $15-25 | 1-2.5% | $10-20 | $40-70 | **4-7%** | DBS/OCBC fee schedules |
| **UAE** | $10-20 | 0.5-1.5% (INR/PKR) | $0-10 | $15-35 | **1.5-3.5%** (to India/PK) | UAE Exchange |
| **UAE** | $25-40 | 2-3.5% (EUR/GBP) | $15-25 | $60-90 | **6-9%** (to EU/UK) | Same banks, different corridor |
| **EU (SEPA)** | €0 | 0% | €0 | $0 | **0%** (to EU) | SEPA regulation |
| **EU (non-SEPA)** | €15-30 | 1.5-3% | €10-20 | $40-70 | **4-7%** (to PH/IN/LATAM) | SWIFT via EU banks |

**Key finding:** UAE→India/Pakistan is 1.5-3.5% — cheap enough that EasyStaff can't compete with banks. UAE→EU/Lebanon/Africa is 6-9% — that's where the pain is. **US is expensive for ALL corridors** (6.5-11%).

### Wise limitations (critical for positioning)

- **Cannot send FROM AED** — UAE businesses cannot use Wise for outbound payments ([source](https://wise.com/help/articles/2932360/guide-to-aed-transfers))
- **Saudi Arabia** — limited to partner bank (SAB) integration, not direct Wise business accounts
- **Qatar** — limited availability
- **Business accounts restricted** in Bahrain, Israel, Malaysia (personal only)

This means UAE/Saudi/Qatar agencies have NO cheap fintech alternative for outbound payments. Banks or Deel are their only options. **EasyStaff fills a real gap here.**

## Recommended Scraping Order (Maximize leads per effort)

### Phase 1 — Immediate (highest ROI)
1. **New York** — Apollo Companies + People scrape, agencies 5-200 employees
2. **Los Angeles** — Same filters, focus on creative/media/production
3. **Miami** — Same, focus on LATAM-connected agencies

**Rationale:** US = 80% of qualified meetings. Wire fees are the worst globally. W-8 compliance is universal pain. Three cities, one country, one outreach language (English), one Deel pricing weakness ($49 is expensive for US SMBs).

**Expected yield:** 4,000-6,000 agency targets → after filtering → 800-1,500 clean leads → 50-100 qualified meetings

### Phase 2 — High-conviction (underserved markets)
4. **Riyadh** — Arabic/English outreach, focus on new agencies
5. **Jeddah** — Same
6. **Doha** — Same

**Rationale:** No Deel, no Wise, restrictive banking. Smaller volume but near-zero competition. Need Arabic outreach capability.

**Expected yield:** 800-1,500 agency targets → 200-500 clean leads → 30-60 qualified meetings

### Phase 3 — Volume (established markets, more competition)
7. **London** — Large volume, need sharp positioning vs Deel/Wise
8. **Singapore** — Focus on agencies paying outside ASEAN
9. **Sydney + Melbourne** — AU-PH corridor + diverse payers

**Expected yield:** 3,000-5,000 agency targets → 600-1,200 clean leads → 40-80 qualified meetings

### Phase 4 — Opportunistic
10. **Berlin + Amsterdam** — Only non-SEPA corridor agencies
11. **Austin** — Small but high-quality
12. **Abu Dhabi** — Diminishing returns vs Dubai

## Critical Filters for Each City

### US cities (NYC, LA, Miami, Austin)
- **Target:** Digital/creative/media agencies, 5-200 employees
- **Exclude:** Large agency networks (WPP, Omnicom, Publicis subsidiaries), pure consultancies, solo freelancers
- **Signal:** "We work with freelancers in..." or team page showing international names
- **Angle:** "You're paying $35-50 per wire + 3% FX to pay each freelancer. With 15 contractors, that's $750-1,500/month just in bank fees."

### Saudi cities (Riyadh, Jeddah)
- **Target:** Digital agencies, production houses, tech startups, 5-100 employees
- **Exclude:** Government entities, oil companies, large Saudi conglomerates
- **Signal:** New companies (2022-2026 founded), expat founders, English website = international team
- **Angle:** "Your bank needs 3 forms per transfer and charges SAR 150. We handle 30 countries in one dashboard."

### Dubai (continue with refinement)
- **Target:** Agencies paying to EU, LATAM, Africa, Lebanon — NOT just India/Pakistan
- **Exclude:** UAE→India/Pakistan-only companies (they don't need us)
- **Signal:** Website mentions multiple countries, non-South-Asian team members, EU/LATAM clients
- **Angle:** "Wise can't send from AED. Banks charge 6-9% to EU. We solve both."

### London
- **Target:** Agencies with non-EU contractors (Eastern Europe, PH, India, Africa)
- **Exclude:** Agencies that only work within UK/EU (SEPA handles those)
- **Signal:** Remote team mentions, international job postings, non-EU client work
- **Angle:** "Post-Brexit, paying your Ukrainian designer costs £25-40 per wire. Deel charges £49/month per head. We're cheaper than both."

### Singapore
- **Target:** Agencies paying outside ASEAN (India, LATAM, Eastern Europe, Africa)
- **Exclude:** Agencies only working within SG/ASEAN (cheap bank transfers)
- **Signal:** International team, non-ASEAN client base
- **Angle:** "DBS charges SGD 20-35 per wire outside ASEAN + 2% FX. 15 contractors = SGD 500+/month wasted."
