EdTech is the rare segment with **high payout-count (thousands of monthly payees) × low per-payee amount ($50-$500)** → matches EasyStaff's transaction-based pricing wedge perfectly, and is anti-ICP-clean (platforms are B2B orgs, not solo creators; GoStudent enterprise 500+ tier is anti-ICP but mid-size 51-200 is fine; Udemy/Coursera solo-instructor model is excluded via platform-vs-instructor distinction).

Non-standard call-out: online tutoring is an industry **not associated with payroll/contractor payments** at the platform-B2B level — existing outreach lists treat EdTech as "education" (anti-ICP gov-school-adjacent) and miss the mid-size global platforms.

### Pain Architecture (Chain-of-Thought)
1. **Current solution:** Payoneer (default for PH/IN/KE/CO/MX tutors — dominant geo), Wise Business for EU tutors, local bank wires for India-resident tutors (easier with Indian platforms like Filo/Vedantu), PayPal fallback, spreadsheet-per-month reconciliation. Some platforms use "in-platform wallet" (tutor earns credits, cashes out monthly) but the cashout rail is still Payoneer underneath.
2. **Point of breakage:** A monthly payout run across 5,000+ tutors × $100 avg = $500K volume, ~20 countries. Payoneer per-tx fees ($3 per pickup + 2-3% FX) eat 5-7% of the tutor's take-home. Tutors quit over "payment shrink" → platform bench replenishment costs. PH tutors face withholding tax compliance complexity (EDGE Tutor blog evidence) → platform loses tutors to cleaner-paying competitors. India TDS (withholding tax) requires per-tutor 194J / 194JB forms → platform Finance team burns 80-150 hours/month just on TDS compliance per year-end.
3. **Cost of pain:** 3-7% payment shrinkage compounds into 10-15% effective tutor-bench erosion annually (platforms lose tutors to the 2-3 competitors offering fractionally better take-home); 80-150 hours/month on compliance + reconciliation; Finance team scaling linearly with tutor count instead of sublinearly (the sign of broken ops).
4. **What EasyStaff eliminates:** Batch payouts to 5,000+ tutors in one run, per-transaction €39 (or 3% for small) → <1% effective on volume → direct tutor take-home improvement → bench retention lift; multi-currency + stablecoin option for Turkey/Argentina/emerging-market tutors; closing docs per payout for platform's own enterprise-client / school-district audits; CIS rails for Russian/Ukrainian STEM tutors (high supply for math/physics/CS platforms).
5. **First objection + answer:** *"Our tutors are all set up on Payoneer - can't ask them to switch."* Answer: *"No switch required. Payoneer stays as an option. For the tutors hitting the 2-3% FX + per-tx fee ceiling on small payouts, we offer a direct-to-local-bank route at <1% effective. They choose. Your bench retention metric is what moves. 15-min demo of the optional-rail setup."*
