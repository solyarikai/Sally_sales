## 4. Turning Points

### "White-label is a feature, not a driver"

All v5 sequences and the active campaign 3096746 are built around white-label as the core angle. But white-label is something clients appreciate after purchase — it rarely drives the purchase decision itself.

Real OnSocial USPs that drive purchases:
- **Volume**: 450M+ profiles (vs 20-50M at competitors)
- **Price**: pay-per-request, no monthly minimums
- **Data quality**: credibility breakdown, city-level demographics
- **Trust**: 9 years in market
- **Social proof**: Whalar, BBB, Lefty, Viral Nation as clients

### "Does anyone really show opinion instead of data?"

Every IM agency already uses data tools. The conversation is not "data vs opinion" — it's **"which data and at what price."** This shifted the angle from educational ("you need data") to competitive ("your data is incomplete and overpriced").

### "I have no idea what happened with Lefty, and neither do you"

The Lefty proof point ("freed 2 engineering roles, expanded from 1 to 5 platforms") was never verified. Building email sequences on unverified stories is a house of cards. Decision: build Email 2 on product facts only, collect real case studies later.

## 6. What's Effective

| Element | Why it works |
|---------|-------------|
| "20M out of 450M" in Creative Email 1 | Concrete contrast in the question itself — not bragging, showing the gap |
| "What happens?" (Account/Ops Email 1) | Open question — forces recipient to imagine their own worst case |
| Mock report / sample report CTA | Tangible value BEFORE the call, low barrier |
| "$40K contract / 60% fake followers" (Re-engagement) | Only concrete verifiable proof across all campaigns |
| "Save 30-40%" (Re-engagement Email 3) | If real — strongest closer available |
| "Sent from my iPhone" (exit email) | Creates casual, personal message feel |
| Product facts instead of stories | Can't dispute: 450M is 450M, 9 years is 9 years |
| Pay-per-request, no minimums | Removes pricing objection before it appears |
| "the coverage question" subject line | Intriguing, doesn't telegraph sales intent |
| No geo hook in Creative | Question itself is strong enough — stat hook was dead weight |

## 7. What's Not Effective

| Element | Why it doesn't work |
|---------|-------------------|
| White-label as central angle | Feature, not purchase driver. Few come looking for this |
| Lefty "freed 2 engineering roles" | Unverified, irrelevant for Creative and Account segments |
| Geo stats without sources (51%, 50%+) | If recipient Googles and finds nothing — trust drops |
| InfluencerUK, LADbible in social proof | Unverified names dilute credible ones |
| Zalando in agency list | E-commerce brand, not agency. Recipient knows this |
| Same Email 2 for three segments | Founders/Creative/Account think about different things |
| {{custom3}} prepared but unused | Dead variable — wasted pipeline work |
| Subject lines like "creator data question" | Describes sender's intent, doesn't create curiosity |
| "67% of brands considering in-house" (3096746) | Another unsourced stat |
| "40% higher retention" (3096746) | Another unsourced stat |
| "Do you show opinion or data?" angle | Condescending — every agency already uses data |
| Identical geo variables across 3 segments | Pain points should differ per role, not just per region |
