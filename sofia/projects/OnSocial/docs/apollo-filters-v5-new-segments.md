## Segment 4 — SOCIAL_MEDIA_MGMT

Social media management and scheduling platforms that need creator/influencer data to expand into influencer marketing, social listening, or competitive intelligence features.

> Why this is a fit: Companies like Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprinklr, and Later (before Later acquired Mavrck) all started as scheduling tools and ADDED influencer features. This is a massive product expansion trend -- SMM platforms need creator data (audience demographics, engagement rates, fraud scores) to compete with dedicated IM platforms. OnSocial's API is the fastest path to adding these features without building data infrastructure from scratch.
>
> Why NOT already covered: Previous searches explicitly EXCLUDED "social media management like Hootsuite or Buffer" and "content scheduling." These are the exact companies we now want to target -- they are buyers, not competitors.
>
> Differentiation from INFLUENCER_PLATFORMS: INFLUENCER_PLATFORMS targets companies that ALREADY have influencer/creator products. This segment targets companies that DON'T yet but are expanding into it. Different pitch: "add influencer features to your existing product" vs "replace your scrapers."

## Segment 5 — ECOM_BRAND_AMBASSADOR

E-commerce and DTC brands running in-house influencer/creator/ambassador programs at scale. These are NOT agencies or platforms — they are the BRANDS that manage their own creator relationships and need data to vet, analyze, and track creators.

> Why this is a fit: Major DTC and e-commerce brands (Gymshark, Fashion Nova, Revolve, Glossier, Fenty Beauty) run ambassador programs with 1,000–50,000 creators. They need audience verification (is this creator's audience real?), demographic data (does this creator reach our target market?), and fraud detection. Many use HypeAuditor or Modash for this — OnSocial can offer the same data via API integrated into their internal tools, or white-label dashboards for their brand marketing teams.
>
> Why NOT already covered: All previous searches targeted TECHNOLOGY companies and AGENCIES. Zero runs targeted the end-brand buyers who run their own programs. This is a net-new buyer persona.
>
> Volume justification: In 2025, 77% of brands with 500+ employees had an in-house influencer function. The DTC boom means thousands of e-commerce brands run ambassador programs.

## Recommended Launch Order

1. **SOCIAL_MEDIA_MGMT** — Highest fit certainty. These companies are actively expanding into influencer features (Later acquired Mavrck, Sprout Social acquired Tagger). The "add creator data to your existing product" pitch is proven.

2. **ECOM_BRAND_AMBASSADOR** — Largest volume, net-new buyer persona (brands, not tech companies). Different sequence needed (marketing leaders, not CTOs). High strategic value: if brands buy directly, agencies feel pressure to upgrade their own tools.

3. **ADTECH_CREATOR_CHANNEL** — Strong trend-driven segment (programmatic influencer is 2026's hottest topic). Slightly lower pass rate (many ad tech companies won't be relevant), but the ones that ARE relevant will be high-value accounts.

4. **GAMING_ESPORTS_PLATFORMS** — Niche but high-intent. Gaming companies spend heavily on creator partnerships. Requires gaming-specific sequence (different language, different pain points). Launch after validating the first 3.
