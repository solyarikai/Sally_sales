# Email outreach

## 1. Email validation

 

- Find emails with **Clay** or **Findymail** . ‣
- **Always** run lists through **MillionVerifier** before sending—even if sourced from trusted tools.
- Why: protects deliverability, lowers bounces, avoids spam issues; tools like **Smartlead** penalize dirty lists.
- Flow: **Source → Verify (MillionVerifier) → Send.**

## Remove old leads from Smartlead

To keep your workspace organized and avoid clutter, make sure to **remove old leads from Smartlead every friday**. Here’s how to do it properly:

1. **Export your leads**
    
    Open the campaign, click **“Download as CSV”** to save the list of leads.
    
    Add this file as a **new sheet** in your project’s lead tracker — this way, you’ll still have access to the data if needed later.
    
    ![image.png](Email%20outreach/image%207.png)
    
2. **Name the sheet after the campaign**
    
    Use the exact same name as the campaign in Smartlead so it’s easy to trace back.
    
3. **Delete all leads**
    
    In the campaign view, click **“Delete all leads”** (see screenshot 3) to clear the list inside Smartlead.
    
    ![image.png](Email%20outreach/image%208.png)
    
4. **Delete the campaign**
    
    Once the leads are removed, **delete the campaign itself** (screenshot 4) to keep your Smartlead dashboard clean.
    
    ![image.png](Email%20outreach/image%209.png)
    

## Guide to Managing Email Responses Outside SmartLead

SmartLead sometimes misses responses from leads, whether due to different email addresses or forwarding errors, leading to lost leads or negative feedback.

**Solution**: Use an external email client (use [**Thunderbird](https://www.thunderbird.net/en-US/) or [Spark](https://sparkmailapp.com/)**).

- Add all work emails to the client.
- Regularly check for replies outside of SmartLead to ensure nothing is missed.

**Why this works**:

- Full visibility of all responses.
- Prevents missed warm replies.

This approach helps keep communication smooth and avoids issues with lost replies!

Make sure to turn off your antivirus email check if you have one to avoid **advertisement subscription** at the bottom of the emails. This happens with Avast antivirus because **settings** include this feature by default.

![image.png](Email%20outreach/image%2010.png)

Go to your **Avast settings** and disable this option to prevent the subscription from being added to your outgoing emails, ensuring better deliverability.
