# LinkedIn outreach

## 1. LinkedIn Setup

### Sync with the Client

Before starting any LinkedIn outreach campaign, align with the client regarding account usage. Sometimes, you will use the client's LinkedIn account, other times you will use your own. Discuss this upfront to ensure everyone is on the same page.

**Important:**

Before launching any campaign, always **check the SSI (Social Selling Index)**. Every time a campaign is launched, **notify the head of operations** with the current SSI and confirm the campaign is ready to go live. This ensures the account is in good standing and optimized for outreach. **Add the SSI to the "SSI" column in the client's Google Sheet** before creating the capacity plan.

### LinkedIn Premium Subscription

Since LinkedIn accounts require a paid premium subscription, discuss the subscription cost with the client. **Ask them to provide the accounts directly or agree on a refund if you’re using your own.** Ensure this is clear from the beginning to avoid any surprises.

### Handling Low SSI

If you notice a low SSI, inform your client:

‣ 

> “I noticed that your Social Selling Index decreased by 2 points (from 49 to 47). This may have happened because we sent a large number of invites, but only 20% were accepted.
> 
> 
> Since this is an important indicator for LinkedIn (affecting message limits and reducing the risk of being blocked), would you consider planning some content marketing activities on your page?
> 
> It would be great if you could post on LinkedIn at least once a week.”

## 2. Getsales Setup

### Proxy Setup

Each LinkedIn account must use its own dedicated proxy. This means buying one proxy per account and always logging in through that proxy — **no exceptions**. This helps keep the account healthy and avoids triggering LinkedIn’s security filters. Regularly track your SSI.

[**Proxy Setup Guide**](https://www.notion.so/Proxy-f978e77cd31a4a17ad16cc5025ad4bc8?pvs=21)

[Adding accounts to getsales](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KXYp_eMlDoE&t=215s)

### Connection Limit

Never send more than **200 connections per LinkedIn account per week**. Exceeding this limit risks restrictions or even bans. Focus on **consistency over volume**. If your SSI is below 30, **limit connections to 100 per week**.

- **Withdrawal Queue:** Ensure you’re reducing the number of pending invitations and withdrawals regularly. A high pending queue can lead to restrictions or even temporary bans. Consider withdrawing invites that aren't accepted after a reasonable period (e.g., 1–2 weeks).
- **Connection Rate:** Your connection conversion rate is excellent, but if the withdrawal queue remains high, it could affect future outreach. Make sure to focus on the balance between invitations and removals.
