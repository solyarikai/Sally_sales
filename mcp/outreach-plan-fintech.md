# Email Outreach Campaign Plan: B2B Fintech Companies

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Goal**: Book 10–50+ qualified discovery calls per month with decision-makers at B2B fintech companies who need predictable pipeline growth.

**Offer**: Sally's done-for-you lead generation — a dedicated GTM team that delivers qualified appointments through omnichannel, signal-driven outreach.

**Timeline**: 12-week rolling campaign with weekly iteration cycles.

## 3. Lead Sourcing Strategy

### Data Sources

1. **Crunchbase / PitchBook** — filter by fintech vertical, funding stage, recent rounds
2. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — build saved searches by title + industry + headcount
3. **Apollo.io / ZoomInfo** — verified emails, technographic filters
4. **BuiltWith / Wappalyzer** — identify tech stack (CRM, marketing automation) to qualify fit
5. **G2 / Capterra** — find fintech companies with active profiles (indicates GTM maturity)
6. **Job boards (LinkedIn Jobs, Wellfound)** — hiring for sales roles = pipeline pain
7. **Conference attendee lists** — Money20/20, Finovate, LendIt, Singapore Fintech Festival

### Intent Signals to Prioritize

- **Just raised funding** (Series A–D in last 90 days) — flush with cash, mandated to grow
- **Hiring SDRs/AEs** — need pipeline but building in-house is slow
- **Competitor users** — companies using Belkins, CIENCE, Martal, or doing outbound poorly
- **Product launches** — new product = new TAM = need outreach
- **Leadership changes** — new CRO/VP Sales often brings new vendors in first 90 days
- **Expanding to new markets** — need local pipeline, don't have local team

### List Size Targets

- **Initial build**: 2,000–3,000 verified contacts
- **Weekly enrichment**: 200–300 new contacts added
- **Validation**: Bounce rate must stay below 3% (use NeverBounce/ZeroBounce)

## 4. Email Infrastructure Setup

### Domain & Mailbox Strategy

| Component | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Domains | 3–5 lookalike domains (e.g., `getsally.co`, `sally-team.io`, `trysally.io`) |
| Mailboxes | 3 mailboxes per domain = 9–15 total sending accounts |
| Daily send limit | 30–40 emails per mailbox per day (never exceed 50) |
| Total daily capacity | 270–600 emails/day across all mailboxes |
| Warmup | 14–21 day warmup period using Instantly or Smartlead warmup networks |
| SPF/DKIM/DMARC | Mandatory on all domains before first send |
| Custom tracking domain | Separate tracking domain per sending domain |

### Deliverability Hygiene

- **Warmup protocol**: Start at 5 emails/day, increase by 3–5/day, reach 30–40/day by day 21
- **List cleaning**: Verify every email before adding to sequence (NeverBounce, ZeroBounce, MillionVerifier)
- **Bounce monitoring**: Pause mailbox if bounce rate exceeds 3% on any given day
- **Spam testing**: Run every new copy variation through mail-tester.com and GlockApps before launch
- **Reply handling**: All replies must be answered within 2 hours during business hours
- **Unsubscribe**: Include one-click unsubscribe link (CAN-SPAM / GDPR compliance)
- **Blacklist monitoring**: Weekly check on MXToolbox for all sending domains

## 6. A/B Testing Plan

### What to Test (Priority Order)

| Element | Variation A | Variation B | Success Metric |
|---------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Subject line** | Lowercase, casual (`pipeline at {{company}}`) | Question-based (`struggling with fintech pipeline?`) | Open rate |
| **Opening line** | Signal-based personalization | Pain-point lead | Reply rate |
| **CTA** | Soft ask (`worth a chat?`) | Direct ask (`free Thursday at 2pm?`) | Positive reply rate |
| **Social proof** | Specific metric (`35 meetings/month`) | Authority proof (`60+ enterprise clients`) | Reply rate |
| **Sequence length** | 4 emails over 14 days | 3 emails over 10 days | Overall conversion |
| **Send time** | Tuesday/Thursday 8–9am local | Tuesday/Thursday 2–3pm local | Open rate |

### Testing Rules

- Minimum 200 recipients per variation before drawing conclusions
- Run each test for at least 2 full weeks
- Only test one variable at a time
- Winner = statistically significant lift (>95% confidence)
- Document every test result in a shared spreadsheet

## 8. Personalization Framework

### Tier 1 — High Value (Top 200 accounts)

- **Research depth**: 15–20 min per prospect
- **Personalization**: Reference specific product, recent news, LinkedIn post, or mutual connection
- **Channel**: Email + LinkedIn + manual touchpoints
- **Example opener**: *"Saw your talk at Money20/20 on embedded payments — the point about bank partnership friction resonated. That's exactly the kind of complex sale where traditional outbound falls flat..."*

### Tier 2 — Mid Value (Next 800 accounts)

- **Research depth**: 5 min per prospect
- **Personalization**: Company-level signal (funding, hiring, product launch) + role-specific pain
- **Channel**: Email + LinkedIn connection request
- **Example opener**: *"Noticed {{company}} just expanded into the UK market — scaling pipeline internationally is one of the trickiest parts of fintech growth..."*

### Tier 3 — Volume (Remaining 2,000+ accounts)

- **Research depth**: Automated enrichment only
- **Personalization**: Industry + role-based dynamic fields ({{industry_pain}}, {{company}}, {{title}})
- **Channel**: Email only
- **Example opener**: *"Most VP Sales at fintech companies tell us the same thing — the product sells itself in demos, but getting enough demos is the problem..."*

## 9. Compliance & Legal

### Regulations to Follow

| Regulation | Requirement | Implementation |
|------------|------------|----------------|
| **CAN-SPAM** (US) | Physical address, unsubscribe link, honest subject lines | Footer template on all emails |
| **GDPR** (EU/UK) | Legitimate interest basis, right to opt out, data processing records | Document legal basis per segment; honor opt-outs within 48 hours |
| **CASL** (Canada) | Implied consent for B2B, must identify sender | Add sender identification; track consent basis |
| **UAE PDPL** | Similar to GDPR; consent or legitimate interest | Same GDPR framework applies |

### Opt-Out Management

- Global suppression list synced across all sending tools
- Process opt-outs within 24 hours (legally required: 10 business days, but faster = better reputation)
- Never re-upload opted-out contacts from any source
- Quarterly suppression list audit

## 12. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Sending too many emails too fast** — ruins domain reputation permanently. Respect warmup timelines.
2. **Generic copy** — "Hi, I help companies grow" gets deleted. Every email must reference a specific signal or pain point.
3. **Buying cheap lists** — low-quality data = high bounce rates = blacklisted domains. Always verify.
4. **Ignoring deliverability** — if 30% of emails land in spam, you're burning budget. Monitor daily.
5. **Not following up on replies** — a 4-hour response delay kills 80% of warm leads. Staff accordingly.
6. **Over-automating personalization** — {{firstName}} is table stakes. Real personalization means you know something about their business.
7. **No clear CTA** — every email needs exactly one ask. Don't give them three options.
8. **Targeting too broadly** — "all fintech companies" is not an ICP. Narrow by stage, size, signal, and sub-vertical.
9. **Testing too many things at once** — one variable per test. Otherwise you learn nothing.
10. **Giving up after 4 weeks** — outbound takes 6–8 weeks to produce reliable data. Commit to 12 weeks minimum.
