## 2. Target Roles (Decision-Making Unit)

### Primary Targets (Run the Studio Selection Process)

| Role | Why Target | Signal They're Active |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **VP / Director of Content Development** | Owns content strategy, writes the brief, runs studio search | New hire = new content strategy incoming |
| **VP / Director of Brand Entertainment** | Drives animation commissioning for brand IP | LinkedIn posts about trade shows (Annecy, Kidscreen) |
| **Head of Animation / Executive Producer** | Evaluates technical capability, reviews reels | Studio visit invitations, production pipeline discussions |
| **SVP / EVP of Entertainment** | Final budget approval at large toy companies | Earnings call mentions of "content investment" |

### Secondary Targets (Influence or Block)

| Role | Why Target | What They Care About |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| **CMO / VP Brand Marketing** | Budget authority at consumer brands. Brand guardian — can veto | Brand consistency, audience alignment, marketing ROI |
| **VP of Licensing & Partnerships** | Content drives licensing revenue. Signs partnership deals | IP ownership terms, territory rights, merchandising splits |
| **VP of eCommerce / DTC** | Animation drives product discovery and sales | Conversion metrics, content → purchase attribution |
| **Creative Director** | Validates creative quality and style match | Showreel quality, style versatility, brand understanding |

### Economic Buyers (Sign the Check)

| Role | When They're the Buyer | Deal Size |
|------|----------------------|-----------|
| **CEO / President** | Companies <$500M revenue | All deals |
| **Division President / EVP** | Large toy companies (Hasbro, Mattel scale) | $1M+ productions |
| **CMO** | Consumer brands (FMCG) where animation is marketing spend | $500K+ campaigns |
| **Head of Studio / Media Division** | Gaming companies with entertainment arms | $2M+ series |

### The Decision Flow

```
Brand/Marketing identifies need for animated content
        ↓
Content Development VP writes brief + budget request
        ↓
CEO/President (or Division EVP) approves budget
        ↓
Content VP + Executive Producer run studio search (RFP or direct outreach)
        ↓
Studios pitch (creative + technical + budget)
        ↓
Brand Management reviews creative direction (VETO gate)
        ↓
Technical evaluation — studio visits, pipeline review
        ↓
Business Affairs negotiates deal terms
        ↓
Final sign-off: CEO/President + Brand VP + Content VP
        ↓
Contract → Production begins
```

## 3. ABM Plan — God-Mode Outreach for a Narrow Market

### 3A. The Reality

- Total addressable companies: **~150–200** across all subsegments

This is NOT a spray-and-pray market. Every touchpoint must be personalized, researched, multi-channel.

### 3B. Trackable Public Signals (Trigger-Based Outreach)

These are observable events that indicate a company is about to commission animation.
Each signal has a **time-to-decision** estimate and a **recommended action**.

#### Tier 1 Signals — Highest Intent (Act Within 48 Hours)

### 3D. Outreach Cadence (Per Account)

**Week 0 — Signal Detected**
- Research the company (30 min): current animation, competitors, gaps, recent news
- Identify 3–5 decision-makers across the DMU
- Personalize opening message referencing the specific signal

**Week 1 — First Touch**
- LinkedIn connection request to VP/Director of Content (personalized note)
- Email to 2–3 contacts (different angles per role)
- Like/comment on their recent LinkedIn posts

**Week 2 — Value Drop**
- Send a 1-page custom brief: "How [Company] could use animation to [specific goal]"
- Include Crayola case study numbers (600M views, 652K subs in 7 months)
- Reference their specific IP: "Imagine Squishmallows with a Crayola-level animation strategy"

**Week 3 — Social Proof**
- Share a relevant industry insight (trade show recap, competitor analysis)
- Soft ask for a 15-min call

**Week 4–6 — Multi-Thread**
- If primary contact hasn't responded, reach the technical evaluator (EP/Head of Production)
- LinkedIn voice message to the champion
- Forward the custom brief to a second contact: "Sharing this with you as well since [reason]"

**Week 8 — Break-up or Event Trigger**
- "Last note for now" email
- OR: invite to upcoming trade show meeting (Annecy, MIPCOM, Kidscreen)

**Ongoing — Quarterly Nurture**
- Industry insights newsletter (not spam — real analysis)
- Case study updates as TheSoul wins new clients
- Re-engage on any new signal

### 3F. Deal Structures to Pitch

| Model | Description | Best For | Sales Cycle |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Work-for-hire** | TheSoul produces, client owns everything | Toy companies wanting full IP control | 6–12 months |
| **Co-production** | Shared IP, shared financing, shared revenue | Mid-tier companies who can't fully fund | 9–18 months |
| **Service + backend** | Lower upfront fee, revenue share on licensing/merch | Companies open to partnership model | 9–18 months |
| **Development deal** | Client funds pilot/bible, option for full series | Risk-averse first-timers | 3–6 months (pilot) |
| **YouTube-first series** | Lower budget, digital-native, TheSoul distributes | Brands wanting fast, measurable results | 2–4 months |

**Lead with the YouTube-first model** — shortest sales cycle, lowest risk for buyer, easiest to prove ROI.
Upsell to full series once trust is established.
