## 3. Contact Gathering Strategy by Platform

### Strategy A: "Diaspora C-Level" Method (YOUR CORE APPROACH)

This is the highest-signal method. The logic:

> **If a C-level at a US/UAE/AU company is from [target country], that company almost certainly has contractors/staff in [target country].**

#### How to execute by platform:

**Apollo.io:**
1. **Company filters**: HQ in [employer country] (UAE, US, AU, CA, UK)
2. **People filters**:
   - Title: CEO, CTO, COO, VP Engineering, VP Operations, Head of HR, Head of People
   - **University/School**: Filter by universities in target contractor country
     - India: IIT, BITS Pilani, NIT, Delhi University, Anna University
     - Pakistan: LUMS, NUST, IBA Karachi, GIKI, UET Lahore
     - Philippines: UP Diliman, Ateneo, De La Salle, UST, Mapua
     - South Africa: UCT, Wits, Stellenbosch, UKZN
     - Mexico: ITESM (Tec de Monterrey), UNAM, IPN, ITAM
     - Argentina: UBA, ITBA, Universidad de Palermo
     - Brazil: USP, Unicamp, PUC-Rio
   - **Name patterns**: Filter by common first/last names from target country (especially for Indian, Pakistani, Arab names in US/UAE companies)
3. **Company size**: 11-500 employees (sweet spot — big enough to have contractors, small enough to not use Deel/Remote.com)
4. **Industry**: Technology, IT Services, Digital Marketing, Fintech, SaaS
5. **Export**: Verified emails → SmartLead campaign

**Sales Navigator (LinkedIn):**
1. **Lead search** with same university filters
2. **Company search**: HQ country + "hiring in [target country]" boolean
3. **Boolean search example for UAE→India**:
   ```
   (CTO OR "VP Engineering" OR "Head of Technology" OR COO)
   AND (IIT OR "Indian Institute" OR BITS OR NIT OR "Delhi University")
   ```
4. **Company page employees**: Once you find a company with Indian C-level, check their team for more India-based employees (confirms contractor relationship)
5. **Export to Apollo/Clay** for email enrichment

**Clay.com:**
1. **Use Clay tables with enrichment**:
   - Start with Apollo/SalesNav export
   - Enrich with: company headcount by location, technologies used, job postings
   - **AI filter**: "Does this company likely hire contractors in [target country]?" — use GPT to score based on enriched data
2. **Job posting signals** (Clay can scrape):
   - Companies posting jobs in target country on LinkedIn/Indeed = strong signal
   - Filter: "remote" + target country location in job posting
3. **Waterfall enrichment for emails**: Clay → Apollo → Hunter → Dropcontact

### Strategy B: "Job Posting" Method

For corridors where diaspora method has limited reach:

**Apollo/Clay:**
1. Filter companies in employer country that have **job postings mentioning target country**
2. Keywords in job postings: "remote", "[country name]", "contractor", "freelancer", "offshore"
3. Technologies hiring for (signals dev outsourcing): specific tech stacks popular in target country

**LinkedIn Job Search → Company List:**
1. Search: `"hiring" "[target country]" "contractor" OR "freelancer"` posted by companies in employer country
2. Export company list → enrich in Clay → find HR/Finance decision-makers

### Strategy C: "Platform Migration" Method

Find companies currently using competitors (Deel, Remote.com, Oyster, Papaya Global):

**Apollo:**
1. **Technographics filter**: Companies using Deel, Remote.com, Oyster HR, Papaya Global
2. These companies definitively have international contractors — they're paying for it
3. EasyStaff pitch: lower fees, more flexible, human support
4. Target: Head of HR, Head of People, CFO, COO

**Clay:**
1. Scrape G2/Capterra reviews of Deel, Remote.com → extract reviewer company names
2. Enrich reviewer companies → find decision-makers
3. These are validated users of competing platforms

**LinkedIn:**
1. People who list "Deel" or "Remote.com" experience in their tools/skills
2. HR professionals who post about "contractor payments" or "international payroll"

### Strategy D: "Outsourcing Marketplace" Method

For Australia→PH, US→LATAM corridors:

1. **Clutch.co**: Scrape companies that have outsourcing partners in target countries
2. **Upwork client companies**: Companies posting large contracts for [target country] freelancers
3. **AngelList/Wellfound**: Startups marking "remote" with team members in target countries

## 6. Key Insights from Reply Analysis

### What makes leads convert:
1. **Companies with EXISTING contractors** — they understand the pain (e.g., ZOTH: "10-12 freelancers in India, UAE, AU, US, Singapore")
2. **Cost-sensitive companies** — xQuic: "open to anything that reduces our cost"
3. **Companies frustrated with current provider** — messaging about "hidden fees" resonates
4. **ME mega-projects** (NEOM) — massive contractor needs, enterprise budgets

### What doesn't work:
1. **Conference spray-and-pray** — Sigma 1432 replies, only 8 interested, many are counter-pitches (PR, media partnerships)
2. **Generic "pay freelancers" messaging** to companies that don't have freelancers yet
3. **Canada corridors** — 10+ campaigns, 0 qualified leads. Needs complete retargeting.

### Messaging recommendations:
- Lead with **specific savings** ("save 70% on commission" resonated in AU→PH)
- Mention **USDT/crypto payout option** for fintech/iGaming audience
- For ME: emphasize **compliance with local labor laws** + speed of onboarding
- For US→LATAM: lead with **nearshoring** angle + "no annual contracts"
